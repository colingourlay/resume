Web Developer - Résumé

Objective

To work for a company which has challenging & interesting problems I can attack using web-based technologies. I'm excited by real-time communication between the server and a wide range of client devices. I have a desire to build web apps that unite and empower the people using them.

Summary

I am a Scottish web developer, currently working in Brisbane, Australia. I'm very passionate about mobile development. My attention is focused on tuning websites and web apps to make them faster, more responsive and better suited to the needs of their users. I mostly work with JavaScript - my latest exporation being the world of server-side JS (thanks to Node.JS) - and would love to apply my skills to innovative products in performance-critical environments.

Following my graduation from university my fiancé and I moved to Australia (her home country), where I began my professional career. We were married in June 2011. We constantly encourage each other to take chances and make the most of our talents.

Through local tech meetups I was introduced to a community of developers who have been a brilliant source of inspiration & knowledge. I've began to work on some of my side-projects in the open, and plan to continue feeding back into the community that has been so generous to me.

Experience

Brisbane, Australia

Web Developer

October 2009 ~ Present

I am currently working with the web technology team at APN, who build and maintain a news platform that supports websites for over 30 regional newspapers and their mobile counterparts, including dedicated apps for business listings, classifieds and user-generated content. As one of the team's front-end developers, I am constantly met with new, interesting challenges, including templating & styling the data returned from the back-end, JavaScript plugin development, working with 3rd-party APIs, tightening our network performance and bringing structure to our ever-growing client-side codebase.

Whenever possible, I challenge myself to take on a bit of back-end work, to ensure I keep in touch with that area of my talents. I've always considered myself a full-stack web developer, and I'm grateful that my team allow me to entertain that notion.

In 2011, another developer and I ran a 12 week HTML/CSS course, open to anyone in the company who could find a use for it. University gave me a firm grounding in user-centered design and software development, but a lot of the skills I now apply to front-end development were self-taught. I thoroughly enjoyed passing on some of the things I've learned, and would gladly do it again.
